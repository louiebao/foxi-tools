There Is Something About Mary

Dan Brown never mentioned it. But consider: the 1998 Farrelly Brothers film follows a string of men — a detective, an architect, a doctor, a stalker — each independently obsessed with the same woman. Each convinced she is extraordinary. Each willing to cross ethical lines to find her.

Her name is Mary.

She turns out to be exactly as remarkable as they believed, and none of them truly deserved her. Sound familiar?

Brown's Mary: Magdalene — the living Grail, hidden in plain sight, protected by a secret society for two thousand years.
The film's Mary: Jensen — chased across America, her secret guarded by a hairdo and a dog named Puffy.

Both: the subject of a grand obsession no one can quite explain.
